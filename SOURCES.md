# Sources & References

## Ancient & Classical Sources

### Egyptian
- *Pyramid Texts* (c. 2400-2300 BCE). References to Bennu bird.
- *Book of the Dead*. Bennu bird associated with Osiris and resurrection.
- *Coffin Texts*. Solar resurrection symbolism.

### Greek
- Herodotus. (c. 450 BCE). *Histories*, Book II, 73. First detailed Greek description of the phoenix.
- Hesiod. (c. 700 BCE). *Catalogue of Women*. Early phoenix references (fragmentary).

### Roman
- Ovid. (8 CE). *Metamorphoses*, Book XV. Poetic account of phoenix life cycle.
- Pliny the Elder. (77 CE). *Natural History*, Book X, 2. Roman natural history account.
- Tacitus. (c. 116 CE). *Annals*, Book VI, 28. Historical discussion of phoenix appearances.
- Claudian. (c. 395 CE). *Phoenix*. Latin poem dedicated to the phoenix.

### Early Christian
- *Physiologus* (c. 2nd-4th century CE). Early Christian bestiary with phoenix allegory.
- Clement of Rome. (c. 96 CE). *First Epistle to the Corinthians*, Chapter 25. Phoenix as resurrection proof.
- Lactantius. (c. 305 CE). *De Ave Phoenice* (The Phoenix). Christian Latin poem.
- Ambrose. (4th century). *Hexameron*. Phoenix in Christian cosmology.
